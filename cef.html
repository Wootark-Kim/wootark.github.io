A New Law?

In the previous topic, I talked about weight and bias. Within an ai's model, this is known as paramters. The more parameter an ai model has, the more "flexibility" it will have in recognizing complex patterns. In other words, bigger ai models have more paramaters that allow it to capture more complex and subtle patterns in data.

The size of an ai's model is important as it has a correlation to it's error rate. To increase the accuracy of an AI's model output, the general solution is to simply train it with more data. This results in it's errorneous output to be dramatically reduced, but it will eventually plateau/level out.

To increase it's accuracy rate beyond just feeding it more data, we can increase the size of the ai's model by providing it with more parameters. The problem is, while this will increase accuracy vs a smaller ai model, it still runs into the same problem where eventually the accuracy will level out. An additional hinderance is the problem that a larger ai model will require more computing power.

Is this line some form of fundamental law or a happenstance limitation caused by a specific approach?
